CHAPTER I

Writing
     is inhibiting.

S  i  g  h  i  n  g, I sit,
scribbling in ink this      pidgin script.
      I     sing with
      nihilistic witticism, disciplining
signs with trifling gimmicks—impish hijinks which highlight stick sigils.

                   Isn't it          glib?
                   Isn't it          chic?

           I fit childish insights within rigid limits,
writing shtick which might instill priggish misgivings in critics blind with hindsight. I dismiss nitpicking criticism which flirts with philistinism. I bitch; I kibitz—
                            griping whilst criticizing dimwits,
                            sniping whilst indicting nitwits,
   dismissing simplistic
                      thinking, in which
                                                                                    philippic wit is still illicit.
